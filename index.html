How it works

A simple, disciplined format so the 90 minutes are calm, direct, and useful.

Small, steady room

Each Bench is 8–10 founders running service businesses in the ₹3–25L/month range. The room is kept small so everyone has space, and the mix is deliberate so conversations stay relevant.

Pre‑submitted problems

Before each session, you share one specific problem—hiring, lead quality, margins, delivery, operations, or anything else that is blocking progress. Vague topics and generic “brainstorming” are filtered out.

Moderated, time‑boxed discussion

A moderator keeps the session grounded. Each problem gets a set amount of time. The goal is not to impress the room, but to unpack the real constraints and options in front of you.

Concrete next steps

You leave with 2–3 clear actions you can take in the next 1–4 weeks—not a 50‑point wishlist. At the next Bench, you’re expected to report back on what you tried and what changed.

  • People mainly looking for clients or sales leads
  • Anyone uncomfortable with giving as much as they get

Why Founders’ Bench exists

Running a services business is often lonely. Most days are spent between clients, team, and numbers—with very few people who actually understand the mix of delivery, cash flow, hiring, and sales you’re juggling.

Generic founder groups are either too early, too large, or too polite to be useful. Panels and keynotes don’t help when you’re facing a specific, immediate decision about a person, a client, or a contract.

Founders’ Bench exists to create a room where serious service founders can talk plainly about what is actually happening in their businesses—without posturing, without selling, and without being “on stage”.

The focus is on outcomes: a smaller set of better decisions over time, made with the benefit of peers who have seen similar situations before. Trust and relevance matter more than volume.
